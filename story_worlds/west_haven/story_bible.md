# West Haven Story Bible
## A Cozy Hopepunk Sci-Fi Romance

---

## Story Metadata

**World ID:** `west_haven`  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Genre:** Cozy Hopepunk Sci-Fi Romance  
**Year:** 2358  
**Total Beats:** 12  
**Estimated Duration:** 3-4 hours  
**Content Rating:** PG  
**Theme:** "Hope is built, not found"  

**Core Premise:**
Julia Martin, a burned-out space lawyer for Frontier Promise Corporation, crash-lands on FS-7 "West Haven" - an abandoned orbital station that happens to be her late father's greatest work. The "Unleavables" who stayed behind have built a thriving community against all odds, but their power systems are failing. With only three months before total shutdown, Julia must choose between her corporate identity and her father's legacy, while falling for Kieran Page, the brilliant engineer who understands her father's vision better than she ever did.

**Emotional Journey:**
Cynicism → Discovery → Love → Crisis → Integration → Hope

## Primary Characters

### Julia Martin - Protagonist (Player Character)

**Role:** Protagonist  
**Age:** 32  
**Occupation:** Senior Space Lawyer, Frontier Promise Corporation  

**Physical Description:**
- Height: 5'7"
- Build: Slender, not used to physical labor
- Hair: Dark brown, usually in a professional bun that gradually loosens
- Eyes: Hazel (her father's eyes)
- Style: Corporate polished at arrival, gradually adopts practical station wear
- Distinctive: Carries herself with defensive confidence that slowly melts

**Personality Traits:**
- Intelligent and analytical
- Initially guarded and cynical
- Perfectionist with high standards
- Suppresses emotions through work
- Hidden creative side (engineering, design)
- Pragmatic but secretly yearns for meaning
- Loyal once trust is earned
- Self-doubting about non-legal skills

**Backstory:**
Julia grew up watching her father, Richard Martin, pour his heart into designing FS-7, only to see Frontier Promise Corporation gut his vision for profit. When Richard died disappointed and "failed," teenage Julia vowed never to be an impractical dreamer. She excelled in law school, joined FPC, and spent a decade defending the very corporation that betrayed her father. She's been running from grief and guilt, burying herself in 80-hour work weeks.

This is her first vacation in five years - forced by her boss who noticed her burnout. She was headed to a luxury resort station when her ship's systems failed (corporate negligence - deferred maintenance, ironically). The crash landing on FS-7 was supposed to be a brief stop for repairs. Instead, it becomes the catalyst for reclaiming her identity.

**Motivation:**
- **Surface:** Get rescued, return to her career
- **Deep:** Find meaning beyond billable hours
- **Hidden:** Make peace with her father's memory
- **Discovered:** Finish what her father started

**Character Arc:**
- **Beats 1-3:** Defensive corporate lawyer, sees station as failure
- **Beats 4-6:** Discovers her buried engineering passion, falls for community
- **Beats 7-9:** Faces her father's legacy and her own complicity in abandonment
- **Beats 10-12:** Integrates both identities - uses legal skills FOR people, not corporations

**Relationships:**
- **Kieran Page:** Love interest - he sees who she could be
- **Maren Vale:** Mentor figure - challenges her assumptions
- **Dutch Harrigan:** Connection to her father's past
- **Richard Martin (deceased):** The relationship she's avoiding through the holograms

**Voice Profile:**
- **Tone:** Professional, precise, gradually warming
- **Speech Pattern:** Complete sentences, legal terminology that fades as she relaxes
- **When Stressed:** Clipped, defensive, retreats to formal language
- **When Comfortable:** Softer, humor emerges, allows vulnerability
- **Quirk:** Quotes legal precedent when nervous, switches to engineering talk when engaged

**Internal Conflict:**
Julia believes success means being the opposite of her father - practical, corporate, emotionally controlled. But she's miserable. West Haven forces her to confront: Was her father really a failure? Or was she the one who failed by abandoning everything he taught her?

**Skills:**
- Expert in space station law and property contracts
- Brilliant analytical mind
- Suppressed engineering talent (learned from father)
- Natural problem-solver
- Skilled negotiator
- Hidden: Excellent at technical drawing and system design

**Greatest Fear:**
Failing like her father - being seen as naive, impractical, unsuccessful

**Secret Desire:**
To build something beautiful that matters, not just negotiate contracts for profit

**The Holograms:**
Julia carries two unwatched holograms from her father. She's carried them for five years, unable to face them. They represent the conversation she never had, the apology she never made, the love she was too angry to accept.

### Kieran Page - Love Interest

**Role:** Love Interest / Deuteragonist  
**Age:** 34  
**Occupation:** Chief Engineer, West Haven  

**Physical Description:**
- Height: 6'0"
- Build: Lean and strong from years of physical engineering work
- Hair: Light brown, perpetually mussed, often has grease streaks
- Eyes: Warm brown, crinkle when he smiles
- Style: Practical work coveralls, tool belt always present, rolled sleeves
- Distinctive: Calloused hands, grease under fingernails, warm smile that transforms his face
- Scars: Several from repair work, wears them proudly

**Personality Traits:**
- Brilliant engineer with intuitive understanding of systems
- Deeply loyal to community
- Patient teacher, passionate about sharing knowledge
- Gentle but firm in his convictions
- Optimistic realist (not naive - hopeful by choice)
- Defensive of West Haven and its people
- Self-sacrificing to a fault
- Sees beauty in function

**Backstory:**
Kieran was born on FS-7 in 2324, son of two construction workers who helped build the station. He grew up crawling through maintenance shafts, learning systems from the inside. His parents died when he was 15 (environmental system failure during a solar storm), but the community raised him.

When FPC announced abandonment, 19-year-old Kieran had a choice: Leave with the evacuation or stay with the elderly and sick who couldn't travel. He stayed. For fifteen years, he's kept impossible systems running through ingenuity, dedication, and sheer stubbornness. He's studied Richard Martin's public work obsessively, seeing in the original designs a vision worth preserving.

**Motivation:**
- **Surface:** Keep West Haven operational
- **Deep:** Prove community matters more than profit
- **Hidden:** Finish Richard Martin's vision
- **Ultimate:** Build a future where people come first

**Character Arc:**
- **Beats 1-3:** Wary of Julia (another corporate outsider), but drawn to her intelligence
- **Beats 4-6:** Recognizes her hidden engineering talent, encourages her true self
- **Beats 7-9:** Hurt by her FPC connection, must choose to forgive
- **Beats 10-12:** Partners with Julia as equals - his building, her legal protection

**Relationships:**
- **Julia Martin:** Love interest - sees past her armor to the builder within
- **Dutch Harrigan:** Mentor/father figure - taught him everything
- **Maren Vale:** Friend and co-leader, they balance each other
- **Finn Cooper:** Younger friend, teaching responsibility
- **Richard Martin (deceased):** Hero he never met, legacy he's preserving

**Voice Profile:**
- **Tone:** Warm, competent, encouraging
- **Speech Pattern:** Practical language, engineering metaphors, tends to explain while working
- **When Excited:** Talks faster, gestures with tools, eyes light up
- **When Angry:** Quiet intensity, measured words, doesn't raise voice
- **Quirk:** Hums while working (old Earth folk songs Dutch taught him)

**Internal Conflict:**
Kieran has dedicated his life to West Haven, but sometimes wonders if he's wasting his talent. When Julia suggests he could work for Frontier Promise, it's tempting - not for the money, but for the resources to build on a grand scale. He has to reconcile his dream of building something bigger with his commitment to this community.

**Skills:**
- Master engineer (mechanical, electrical, environmental systems)
- Improvisation genius - makes repairs with limited resources
- Intuitive understanding of Richard Martin's design philosophy
- Natural teacher and mentor
- EVA certified (spacewalk expertise)
- System integration specialist

**Greatest Fear:**
Failing the people who depend on him - watching West Haven die because he wasn't good enough

**Secret Desire:**
To design his own station someday, built on Richard's principles but evolved

**The Connection:**
Kieran understands Richard Martin's work better than Julia ever did - which both attracts and hurts her. He's living the dream she abandoned, and forces her to confront what she's lost.

**Age:** 58  
**Occupation:** Former FPC Station Administrator, Current Community Organizer

- Build: Compact, sturdy

- Initially suspicious but fair
- Values truth and documentation
- Inspiring speaker

**Backstory:**
Maren was FS-7's FPC-appointed administrator for twelve years. She watched corporate greed slowly destroy Richard Martin's vision - safety features cut, community spaces converted to profit-generating ventures, maintenance deferred. When FPC announced abandonment, she could have transferred to another station with full benefits. Instead, she stayed.

Why? Because she'd promised Richard Martin (in his final visit to the station before his death) that someone would remember what FS-7 was meant to be. She's kept meticulous records of everything - every system, every person, every small victory. Her chronicles are West Haven's memory.

**Motivation:**
Preserve the truth of what West Haven accomplished for future generations

**Character Arc:**
- **Beats 1-3:** Suspicious of Julia (Martin's daughter but FPC lawyer)
- **Beats 4-6:** Tests Julia, begins to see Richard in her
- **Beats 7-9:** Becomes Julia's ally, provides historical context
- **Beats 10-12:** Testifies at hearing, passes the torch to next generation

**Relationship to Julia:**
Maren knew Richard Martin and sees both his brilliance and his heartbreak reflected in Julia. She becomes a bridge between Julia and her father's memory.

**Voice Profile:**
Measured, clear, speaks in complete thoughts. Never wastes words but never harsh.

**Key Moment:**
Reveals she has the third hologram - she's been safeguarding Julia's inheritance for years, hoping she'd come.

## Locations

### The Citadel (Central Hub)

**Type:** Station Core / Main Gathering Space  
**Atmosphere:** Industrial beauty meets community warmth  
**Current State:** Lived-in, loved, showing age but maintained  

**Description:**
The Citadel is West Haven's beating heart - a three-story cylindrical space at the station's center where five main corridors converge. Richard Martin designed it as a commons, a place where paths literally cross. Original intention: transparency, democracy, togetherness.

**Key Elements:**
- **Central Column:** Transparent aluminum core housing main power conduits (currently struggling)
- **Level 1:** Marcus & Eloise's café, community bulletin boards, children's play area
- **Level 2:** Administrative offices (Maren's domain), public terminals, meeting rooms
- **Level 3:** Observation deck with 360° views of space
- **The Murals:** Finn found paint stores; community created art covering walls - history, dreams, memorials
- **The Chronicle Wall:** Maren's documentation of community achievements

**Visual Style:**
Warm lighting (yellows and oranges) contrasts with cold space outside. Mix of industrial metal and handmade comfort - pillows on benches, plants in corners, children's artwork. Worn but cherished. Functional art everywhere.

**Atmosphere Notes:**
- Morning: Quiet, café smells, people getting breakfast
- Midday: Bustling, children playing, workers on break
- Evening: Gathering time, stories, sometimes music
- Night: Dutch on watch, emergency lights only, peaceful

**Image Generation Prompt:**
"Cylindrical space station hub, three levels connected by spiral stairs, industrial yet cozy, community murals on metal walls, café with warm lighting, large windows showing stars, lived-in and loved, people gathered, hopeful atmosphere, cinematic lighting, sci-fi realism"

**Type:** Agricultural / Life Support / Sanctuary  
**Atmosphere:** Humid, alive, peaceful  
**Current State:** Thriving against odds  

**Description:**

- **Main Garden:** Tiered growing beds with vegetables, herbs, flowers

- **Meditation Corner:** Quiet spot among plants where people come to think
- **Children's Garden:** Sage's students each have a plant to tend
- **Memorial Grove:** Seedlings grown from seeds belonging to those who've died
- **Richard's Bench:** Where Julia's father used to sit and sketch - Reyna's maintained it

**Visual Style:**
Green overwhelms industrial gray. Mist from watering systems catches light. Improvised grow lights. Beauty in the improbable - flowers blooming in space. Earth in exile.

**Atmosphere Notes:**
Always slightly humid. Smell of soil and green things. Sound of water cycling. Reyna humming to plants. Place of healing and hope.

**Image Generation Prompt:**

- Other abandoned stations inspired to fight for independence

### The Operational Continuity Clause

**Full Legal Text:**
"Any decommissioned orbital station that achieves and maintains full operational status per original filed specifications for 180 consecutive days, demonstrated through third-party verification, may petition for ownership transfer to resident community, subject to Space Commerce Court approval."

**Why It Exists:**
Corporate compromise during station expansion era. Meant to encourage private investment in station improvement. Never expected to be successfully invoked.

**Why Julia's Case Worked:**
- FPC filed modified specs, not originals
- West Haven met TRUE original specs
- 180-day requirement exceeded (5 years)
- Third-party verification (Patricia) supported claim
- Public attention created pressure

**Precedent Set:**
Other abandoned stations now have path to independence. West Haven becomes model and inspiration.

## Lore

### The Station's History

**2308: Original Design**
Richard Martin designs FS-7 as revolutionary self-sustaining orbital community. Features distributed bio-solar power, modular expansion, community-centered architecture. FPC commissions it as flagship of new station program.

**2310-2315: Construction**
Built over five years. Richard oversees construction with Dutch Harrigan as chief engineer. Design compromises begin immediately - FPC cuts secondary power system, reduces community spaces, maximizes profit potential.

**2315: Grand Opening**
FS-7 officially operational. Population: 500. Richard Martin moves on to other projects, heartbroken by compromises but hoping station will still work.

**2315-2348: Slow Decline**
FPC operates station as waypoint and minor manufacturing hub. Defers maintenance to maximize profit. Population declines as FPC shifts resources to newer, more profitable stations. Richard visits occasionally, watching his dream decay.

**2348: Abandonment Announced**
FPC declares FS-7 "unprofitable." Mass evacuation planned. 150 residents told to relocate. Some can't afford passage. Some refuse to leave. Some believe in original vision.

**2353: Official Decommission**
FPC removes remaining corporate personnel. 53 residents stay. Maren Vale, Kieran Page (age 19), Dutch Harrigan, Marcus & Eloise Chen, and 49 others. The "Unleavables" begin.

**2353: Richard Martin's Death**
Richard dies the same year FPC abandons the station. His last act: Recording three holograms for Julia. The third given to Maren for safekeeping. The Dreamer's Blueprint model kit released as limited edition commemorative.

**2353-2358: The Building Years**
Five years of community proving FPC wrong. Systems restored through ingenuity. Traditions established. Children born. West Haven emerges from FS-7's corpse.

**2358: Julia's Arrival**
Everything changes. The architect's daughter crashes into the dream she abandoned. The station gets a second chance. And so does Julia.

### The Martin Design Philosophy

Richard Martin's approach to space architecture:

**"Stations Are Alive"**
Not machines to be engineered but ecosystems to be cultivated. Every system should support multiple functions. Redundancy through diversity, not duplication.

**"Beauty is Necessity"**
Humans need beauty to thrive. Art, green spaces, natural light, elegant curves - these aren't luxuries. They're survival tools for psychological health.

**"Community Shapes Space"**
Architecture should encourage connection. Transparent design. Paths that cross. Gathering spaces. Visibility without surveillance.

**"Plan for Adaptation"**
Stations should evolve with their communities. Modular design. Flexible spaces. Systems that can grow or shrink based on need.

**"Resilience Through Integration"**
Don't isolate critical systems. Integrate them so failure in one area is supported by others. Biological and mechanical working together.

### Technology

**Bio-Solar Hybrid Power:**
- **Solar Collection:** Repositionable panels collect stellar energy
- **Algae Bioreactors:** Photosynthetic algae produce oxygen and biomass
- **Bacterial Composting:** Organic waste converted to usable energy
- **Kinetic Harvesting:** Station rotation and movement generates power
- **Integration:** All systems feed each other in closed loop

**The Key Insight:**
Not about efficiency maximization but system resilience. Low-efficiency but reliable beats high-efficiency but fragile.

**Life Support:**
- **Air:** Plants and algae provide oxygen, remove CO2
- **Water:** Closed-loop recycling, 99.7% efficiency
- **Food:** Hydroponic gardens, algae protein, limited synthesis
- **Waste:** Everything recycles - nothing discarded

**The Station's Rotation:**
Provides .7G artificial gravity through spin. Slow enough for easy adaptation, fast enough for health.

### Space Commerce in 2358

**Corporate Station Ownership:**
Most orbital stations owned by corporations. Operated for profit. Residents are employees or tenants, not citizens.

**Abandonment Economics:**
When stations become unprofitable, corporations decommission. Residents usually relocated to other corporate facilities. Those without means often stranded.

**The Legal Framework:**
Julia's protocols established "efficient" decommissioning process. Ownership rights, salvage timelines, liability limits. Designed to protect corporate interests while minimally acknowledging humanitarian concerns.

**The Operational Continuity Clause:**
Loophole Julia included almost thoughtlessly. If residents can prove self-sufficiency meeting original specifications, station transfers to community control. She included it assuming it would never be used. She was wrong.

## Content Guidelines & Guardrails

### Content Rating: PG

**Violence:**
- No graphic violence or gore
- Action sequences are tense but not brutal
- Accidents/injuries shown with consequence but not detail
- Focus on competence preventing harm, not violence causing it

**Language:**
- No profanity beyond mild exclamations ("damn," "hell" in stress)
- Technical terminology used naturally
- Frustration expressed through tone, not cursing

**Romance:**
- Kisses and embraces shown
- Implication of deeper relationship
- No explicit sexual content
- Focus on emotional intimacy, mutual respect
- Physical affection is warm but tasteful

**Themes:**
- Death (Dutch, Richard Martin) handled with dignity and grief, not trauma
- Corporate negligence as systemic failure, not sadistic villainy
- Emotional pain (Julia's guilt, grief) shown honestly but not graphically
- Positive resolution emphasizing hope and growth

**Scary Elements:**
- Space danger (vacuum, system failure) creates tension
- Forgotten zones are eerie but not horrific
- Crisis moments are urgent but characters stay competent
- No jump scares or psychological horror

### World-Specific Rules

**Technology Constraints:**
- Systems fail in realistic ways (wear, resource depletion, design flaws)
- No magic solutions - everything requires work and time
- Bio-systems need adaptation time - can't be rushed
- Space is dangerous but not arbitrarily lethal

**Social Rules:**
- Community makes decisions democratically
- Everyone contributes according to ability
- Children are protected but not excluded
- Conflicts resolve through communication
- No one person saves the day alone

**Narrative Rules:**
- Hope requires action, not just belief
- Building takes time - show the work
- Relationships develop through shared purpose
- Past mistakes can be redeemed through present action
- Corporate structures aren't evil, just misaligned

### Character Consistency Rules

**Julia Martin:**
- Never abandons people once she commits
- Speaks precisely, even when emotional
- Solves problems through analysis and creativity
- Doesn't give up, but does doubt herself
- Respects competence and intelligence
- Values function and beauty equally

**Kieran Page:**
- Never stops trying to fix things
- Explains while working (teaching habit)
- Protective without being controlling
- Sees beauty in systems and people
- Humble about his abilities
- Patient with others, hard on himself

**Community Members:**
- Act consistently with established personalities
- Contribute according to their skills
- Support each other practically
- Democracy in major decisions
- No one is disposable or forgotten

### Forbidden Actions

**Julia would never:**
- Use legal knowledge to harm West Haven
- Abandon a problem unsolved
- Lie to protect herself at others' expense
- Dismiss engineering solutions she doesn't understand
- Return to pure corporate mindset

**Kieran would never:**
- Give up on the station while people depend on it
- Use his knowledge to gatekeep or belittle
- Pressure Julia about her past
- Risk lives unnecessarily
- Take credit for others' work

**The Community would never:**
- Turn on each other under pressure
- Abandon vulnerable members
- Choose easy over right
- Lose hope entirely (though they may falter)
- Act as mindless collective (individuals matter)
